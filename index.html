COVID-19 Vaccine Distribution Model

By Hackerknights

The project will provide data modelling for how to effectively distribute COVID-19 vaccines. This will provide the Government with a tool to send out resources to the exact location targeting specific areas with a specific amount of immunisation targets, allowing them to use their resources with utmost efficiency. Vaccines will be limited because of the high demand that they will carry. Deciding on where and who will need to be vaccinated to effectively stop the spread of the virus and to reach herd immunity.

How to:

  1. 1. Default figure: Vaccine for all months
  2. 2. Select month from bottom to get a specific month
  3. 3. Enter number of vaccine available
  4. 4. Piechart shows distribution on region based on available vaccines

Background:

This model takes into consideration COVID cases that have been confirmed and deaths per state over a time period to calculate the R naught value. These cases have been forecasted up to march 21 using multiple linear regression.

After calculating R naught found to be 2.5 on average. We calculated the number of vaccines needed to reach herd immunity for each state according to its population. The formula for Herd immunity: 1 / (1 - Rnaught) We have run this algorithm to our forecasted range of Mar 21.

If we get a limited number of vaccines, the algorithm calculates the optimal distribution of vaccines to take smaller steps towards herd immunity (no more a pandemic).
